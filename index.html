Education for Wellbeing

Why education matters and how people and groups make change. For Year 5 and 6 students.

Overview Australia India Take Action Bibliography

What is the issue?

Education is more than school. It helps people learn skills, get jobs and make healthy choices. When children can’t go to school, their lives are harder. Some communities, especially Indigenous or very remote groups, face extra barriers.

Why it matters

  • Better education → more job opportunities.
  • Education helps keep communities healthy and safe.
  • Girls and Indigenous students often face extra challenges.

Barriers

  • No nearby school or safe travel to class.
  • Not enough teachers or learning materials.
  • Cultural differences that make school feel unrelated to family life.

Case study: Australia (Aboriginal communities)

Many Aboriginal students in remote areas have less access to high-quality schooling. Distance, poor facilities, and cultural barriers can lower attendance.

Programs that help include the Clontarf Foundation (which supports Aboriginal boys through school) and government attendance programs that partner with communities.

Quick classroom activity
  1. Discuss: What would make school easier for a child living far from town?
  2. Map task: Mark where local schools are and imagine ways to travel safely.

Case study: India

In many rural parts of India, children miss school because of poverty, long travel times, or family duties. Girls are especially affected.

NGOs like Pratham run learning camps and extra classes to help children catch up with reading and maths.

Classroom discussion prompt

Why might a family choose to keep a child home instead of sending them to school? Think about jobs, chores and safety.

How NGOs and Programs Help

Groups and governments help by building schools, training teachers, creating travel support and designing culturally safe programs for Indigenous students.

  • Mobile schools or teacher visits for remote communities.
  • After-school learning camps to catch up on reading and maths.
  • Programs that work with families and Elders to include culture in lessons.

What can young people do?

  • Share what you learn with friends and family.
  • Run a small fundraiser for a local literacy program.
  • Invite a local Elder or community member to talk about culture in school.
